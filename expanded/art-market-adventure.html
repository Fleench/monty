The Art Market Adventure

Mistaking a restaurant mural for a lost Monet became Monty's favourite performance piece, transforming an error into curated commentary.

Acquisition Drama

ID: AMA-01 • Date: March 12, 2011

Monty staged a midnight “rescue” of the mural, complete with velvet ropes and a narrated backstory about Monet's secret Boston period.

Performance Art Pivot

ID: AMA-02 • Date: March 14, 2011

When authenticity faltered, he declared the piece an experiential installation. Guests wore monocles to observe “capitalist reflection in acrylic.”

Mural's Afterlife

ID: AMA-03 • Date: June 2, 2012

The mural now tours corporate lobbies accompanied by a docent who recites Monty’s original sales pitch. Donations benefit aspiring art appraisers.

Collector Summit

ID: AMA-04 • Date: March 20, 2011

Monty convened a dawn cappuccino summit with friendly curators, distributing certificates attesting to the mural’s “emotional provenance” and promising first refusal on the touring rights.

Restoration Residency

ID: AMA-05 • Date: July 18, 2013

He sponsored an artist-in-residence program to “restore” the piece with theatrical lighting cues, turning each reveal into a staged apology-performance for high-paying audiences.
