Corporate Conquests

Leveraged deals, surprise layoffs, and theatrical turnarounds became Monty's signature moves during the eighties buyout boom.

The Cutlery Incident

ID: CCO-01 • Date: April 4, 1986

Monty replaced sterling forks with plastic for a week to expose “wasteful habits.” The VP who complained first became the example of fiscal discipline.

Morale Rebranding

ID: CCO-02 • Date: September 17, 1987

He coined “curated austerity” to describe layoffs paired with catered motivational breakfasts. Share price rose; employee loyalty did not.

Silver Parachutes

ID: CCO-03 • Date: January 9, 1989

Executives who survived restructuring received engraved parachute pins signifying their loyalty. Several now sell for high prices at discreet auctions.

Hostile Harmony Plan

ID: CCO-04 • Date: May 23, 1988

During a takeover, Monty hired a string quartet to play in the lobby while the merger team issued pink slips, branding the campaign “hostile harmony.”

Legacy Playbook Release

ID: CCO-05 • Date: March 11, 1991

He later compiled tactics into a gilded playbook for protégés, complete with flowcharts on when to replace executives versus rename departments.
